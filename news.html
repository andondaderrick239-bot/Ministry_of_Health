About Ebola

Ebola virus disease is a serious infectious disease caused by the Ebola virus. It spreads through contact with infected body fluids and requires quick medical attention.

The Ministry of Health works with health workers, communities, and partners to control outbreaks through education, testing, treatment, and prevention.

News

Uganda is continuing to respond to an ebola outbreak caused by Bundibugyo Ebola virus.The outbreak was confirmed in Uganda in May 2026 after an imported case linked to neighbouring Democratic Republic of Congo.The Bundibugyo species of ebola involved is one of which their is no vaccine or specific treatment, though work is ongoing to test promising candidates. The outbreak is occuring in a challenging context:humantarian crisis and a remote and densely populated area, combined with insecurity and a high population and trade movements.

Public Awareness Campaigns

  • Community health education programs
  • Radio and television awareness messages
  • Training of health workers
  • Encouraging early reporting of suspected cases
  • Promoting hygiene practices

Symptoms of Ebola

  • Fever
  • Severe headache
  • Muscle pain
  • Weakness and tiredness
  • Vomiting and diarrhea
  • Unexplained bleeding in severe cases

How to Prevent Ebola

  • Avoid contact with infected body fluids
  • Wash hands regularly with soap and clean water
  • Report suspected cases immediately
  • Follow guidance from health authorities
  • Avoid handling bodies of people who died from Ebola
